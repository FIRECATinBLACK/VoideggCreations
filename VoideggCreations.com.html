FDM or SLA?

To figure out whether FDM or SLA is the right choice for you you've got to know what your expectations are and what you want

The advantages of

SLA Printing

SLA (Stereolithography) / Resin 3D printing while currently less commonly used than FDM printing will offer you the visually best possible results with absolutely outstanding and flawless detail for figurines, accessories, statues or anything else you can imagine -Its capable of significantly greater detail than any FDM printer
-You usually cant even tell its printed with the bare eye
-SLA is capable of printing a lot faster than most FDM printers

FDM Printing

FDM (Fused Deposition Modeling) 3D printing is the most common form of 3D printing today and generally a great option if you want a cheap mechanical part or item that doesnt have to look perfect -Its significantly cheaper to print
-The parts can be bigger without having to be split up into seperate pieces
-Most of the time FDM printed parts are stronger than SLA printed ones

Frequently asked Questions

Q: Are 3D prints Fragile?
A: This varies a lot from print to print, a stronger print may be slower to add extra material to increase the prints overall strenght, the opposite is true when prioritizing low cost and high speed printing but overall if you consider being able to toss a print with no damage against a wall strong then no, they are not fragile Q: Why is Resin/SLA printing so much more expensive?
A: Higher material, maintenance, processing and equipment costs.
While for FDM printing the process usually just involves getting your 3d model on the printer, letting it print and then package it to send it to you for resin the production process is a lot more complicated.
Not only does the average kilo of resin cost more than a kilo of most filaments but in order to safely handle a fresh resin print it also has to be washed in IPA or resin away, safety equipment like masks and gloves and protential printing failures all have to be accounted for as well as the amount of time consumed post processing these prints by hand.
